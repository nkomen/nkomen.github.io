Personal Reflection

Much of my life has led up to educating those around me. In order to keep myself afloat while living abroad I taught english. It started as a way to make money, but I found I enjoyed finding unique and interesting ways to communicate with my students. When I would visit home, I would put together videos or slideshows to try to encapsulate my experiences into a few minutes. It was a way for me to share my experiences, but also to invite others into what I had learned in my time away. I found I enjoyed using media--photos, videos, audio, and more--to portray these experiences.

As my time living abroad came to an end I began to fully pursue my undergraduate degree. Something about the sciences drew me, so I started as a biology major. After two years, I realized that while I had enjoyed what I had learned, it wasnâ€™t ultimately going to be for me. I spoke to many professors I had built relationship with and they agreed that shifting gears would probably be most appropriate. I was encouraged to pursue writing. After graduation, I pursued jobs that would would allow me to write. I eventually ended up Costco which quickly led me to joining their Content team.

My time on the content team was extremely valuable. My teammates and I held our work to a high standard which allowed us to learn from one another and produce high quality work. This created more opportunities within the call center and I was able to be part of two major projects--redesigning the Costco Customer Service website, and building standard operating procedure websites for the various departments in the call center.

These experiences and projects made me want more. When these projects came to an end, I decided to move on in hopes of new projects that would give me similar expereinces. I joined a small business that had been recently purchased by a new owner who wanted to make some changes. Not all the changes we talked about came to light, but I have still found my time valuable. Pursuing my Master of Science with the Learning Technology and Design program will I think funnel much of my experience into bettering my skills so that I can move full time into instructional design.

Looking back, I see a lot of my experiences attributing to my current abilities. My background in teaching various ages, cultures, and languages has created a wide familiarity in best communication practices. Being familiar with the scientific method and scientific reasoning has allowed me to be detail oriented and clear when developing instructions.

Writing is a very valuable skill when it comes to instructional design. Then my experiences working on the content development team at Costco and working at a small business that is built around classes and teaching adult learners. I look forward to continuing to build up my abilities to pursue work that truly interests me.
